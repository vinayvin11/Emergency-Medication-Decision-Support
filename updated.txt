Here is a breakdown of the recommended diseases or conditions typically treated by the drugs listed:

Azithromycin: Used to treat bacterial infections such as bronchitis, pneumonia, sexually transmitted infections (e.g., chlamydia, gonorrhea), and infections of the ears, lungs, sinuses, skin, and throat.

Tramadol: Prescribed for moderate to severe pain relief. It is an opioid analgesic.

Ursodiol: Used to treat and prevent certain types of gallstones or to treat primary biliary cirrhosis (a liver disorder).

Chloroquine: Traditionally used to treat or prevent malaria. It is also used for certain autoimmune diseases, such as rheumatoid arthritis and lupus.

Fluconazole: An antifungal medication used to treat and prevent fungal infections, including yeast infections of the mouth, throat, esophagus, and other parts of the body.

Pseudoephedrine: Used as a decongestant to relieve nasal or sinus congestion caused by colds, allergies, and hay fever.

Omeprazole: Commonly used to treat gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), stomach ulcers, and other conditions caused by excess stomach acid.

Metformin: Primarily used to treat type 2 diabetes by helping control blood sugar levels.

Cetirizine: An antihistamine used to relieve allergy symptoms such as runny nose, sneezing, itching, and watery eyes.

Acyclovir: An antiviral medication used to treat infections caused by certain viruses, including herpes simplex virus (causing cold sores and genital herpes), chickenpox, and shingles.
